A tribute to the 'Missile Man' of India

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Abdul Kalam's quote on dreams

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen “A. P. J.” Abdul Kalam was the 11th President of India, serving from 2002 to 2007. Kalam was born and raised in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, and studied physics and aerospace engineering.

He spent the next four decades as a scientist and science administrator, mainly at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), and was intimately involved in India’s civilian space program and military missile development efforts.

    Facts

  • 1931 - APJ Kalam was born into a poor family. His father had only one boat to support his family.
  • 1954 - Kalam completed his graduation from Saint Joseph’s College, Tiruchirappalli.
  • 1955 - He enrolled at the Madras Institute of Technology.
  • 1960 - Kalam joined the Aeronautical Development Establishment of the Defence Research and Development Organisation.
  • 1970 - 1990 - Kalam made an effort to develop the Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV) and SLV-III projects, both of which proved to be successful. Kalam was known as the ‘Missile Man’ of India due to his continued, successful work with ballistic missiles and launch vehicle technology.
  • 2000 - Kalam published 'Wings of Fire'. An autobiography that gives a detailed account of his childhood, his personal life and career.
  • 2002 - 2007 - Kalam served as the 11th President of India. He was known as “the People’s President” because of his simple and down-to-earth attitude.
  • 2003 and 2006 - He was awarded the MTV Youth Icon award twice.
  • 27 July 2015 - Kalam travelled to Shillong to deliver a lecture on "Creating a Livable Planet Earth" at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong. While climbing a flight of stairs, he experienced some discomfort, but was able to enter the auditorium after a brief rest. He was rushed to the hospital but soon passed on due to cardiac arrest.

Kalam was the third President of India to have been honored with a Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honor. He was one the most loved presidents and will be dearly missed. His dream and vision of a better tomorrow will live on in our hearts for eternity.
